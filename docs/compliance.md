# Compliance Notes

This document captures the compliance posture for the P&F + Fundamental Stock Screening Bot. Wealth-management work is heavily regulated; even an internal-only tool deserves explicit attention to scope, recordkeeping, and disclaimers. **None of what follows is legal or compliance advice** — the actual compliance officer at the advisor's firm is the authority. This document records the design choices and the questions that compliance review needs to validate.

---

## Scope (defining what this bot is, and what it isn't)

| Aspect | Posture |
|---|---|
| **Audience** | The advisor, internally. No one else. |
| **Output** | A periodic report (PDF email) of pre-breakout stock candidates with rationale. |
| **What it is NOT** | Not investment advice. Not a recommendation to clients. Not a trade-execution system. Not a client-facing artifact. Not a marketing communication. |
| **Decision-making** | The advisor retains full discretion. The bot surfaces ideas; the advisor decides what (if anything) to do with them. |
| **Trade execution** | None. The bot does not place trades, does not connect to a broker, does not have any execution capability. |

This scope is deliberately conservative. Compared to alternative project shapes (e.g., a robo-advisor, a client-facing dashboard, an auto-trader), this design **avoids triggering most of the regulatory machinery** around investment advice, marketing, and execution.

---

## Why the internal-only scope matters

Several regulatory regimes apply differently — or not at all — when an output is internal versus client-facing:

| Regime | Internal use | Client-facing use |
|---|---|---|
| **FINRA Rule 2210** (Communications with the Public) | Generally not implicated by internal research notes | Likely applies; requires principal approval, filing, and retention |
| **SEC Marketing Rule (Rule 206(4)-1)** | Not implicated | Strict requirements on testimonials, performance presentations, and substantiation |
| **Reg BI** (Regulation Best Interest) | Doesn't apply at the tool level — applies to the recommendation made *by* the advisor *to* the client | The advisor's recommendation is still subject to Reg BI, but the bot's output is one input among many |
| **SEC Rule 17a-4** (Books and records) | Applies in spirit — advisor workpapers should be retained | Stronger requirements if the output is shared with or used as the basis for client advice |

Keeping the bot internal-only is the single biggest compliance simplification this project makes. **Any pressure to make the output client-facing should trigger a fresh compliance review before that change is implemented** — it changes the regulatory profile of the artifact.

## Data licensing — Dorsey Wright

The DWA Terms of Use prohibit scraping the platform under any circumstances, even for internal use. The full research and the safe-access paths are documented in [research/dwa-access.md](research/dwa-access.md). The compliance-relevant summary:

- **Never scrape** dorseywright.nasdaq.com or systems.dorseywright.com. This is a contractual breach and creates direct liability for the firm.
- **Manual CSV exports** from the platform UI are explicit features and are legitimate to use.
- **Licensed API access** (Nasdaq Data Link NDW databases) is legitimate and the highest-fidelity route, at incremental cost.
- **Replicating the methodology from raw OHLC** is legitimate — the math is public and the inputs (price data) are independently licensed.

The current proposed path is **replication from raw OHLC** ([OQ-002](02-open-questions.md#oq-002-dwa-access-path)) — which avoids any DWA-licensing question entirely. The OHLC vendor's terms (Polygon.io, Tiingo, etc.) must still be reviewed.

## Disclaimers on every report

Every report the bot produces must carry a clear disclaimer block. Working text:

> **DISCLAIMER.** This report is an internal research aid generated by an automated screening tool. It is not investment advice, is not a recommendation to buy or sell any security, and is not intended for distribution to clients or any party other than the advisor it is addressed to. The methodology applied here uses Point and Figure technical analysis and a fundamental quality filter; past performance of the methodology is not indicative of future results. All investment decisions remain the advisor's responsibility and must be made consistent with the advisor's fiduciary obligations to clients and the firm's policies.

This text should be approved by the firm's compliance officer before the first report is sent.

## Recordkeeping

SEC Rule 17a-4 and analogous rules require investment advisers to retain certain records. While an internal research tool's outputs aren't strictly "communications with clients" or "recommendations," it is good practice — and may be required by firm policy — to retain:

1. **Every report generated.** Date, recipient (the advisor), contents, the input parameters that produced it.
2. **The methodology configuration as of each report date.** If the composite scoring weights change, the report archive must record which weights were active.
3. **The signal-state-of-the-universe for each report date.** The full universe with each ticker's P&F state, RS rank, fundamental gate result, etc. This is what makes "why was XYZ recommended on date D?" answerable months later.
4. **Data refresh logs.** Which data vendors were queried, when, with what response.

The audit log is built into the design (Phase 5 deliverable). Retention policy length should match the firm's existing recordkeeping retention (typically 5–7 years for investment advisers).

## Items requiring compliance officer sign-off

Tracked in [OQ-006](02-open-questions.md#oq-006-compliance-officer-sign-off):

1. Confirm the internal-only scope is acceptable under firm policy and doesn't trigger additional registration or disclosure.
2. Approve the disclaimer text (or provide preferred language).
3. Confirm the retention policy for report archives matches firm requirements.
4. Confirm the DWA data-access path (whichever option is selected) is consistent with firm policy and the firm's existing DWA license.
5. Confirm the OHLC and fundamentals vendor terms are acceptable.
6. Confirm there are no firm-specific restrictions on systematic idea generation tools.

This sign-off should be documented in writing and stored with the project records. The conversation with compliance is itself a record that may be referenced later.

---

## What this tool is NOT a substitute for

A note for the developer and advisor alike: this bot is a screening aid. It does not replace:

- The advisor's fiduciary duty to clients.
- The advisor's own judgment about which surfaced candidates actually fit a client's situation.
- The firm's existing compliance and supervision framework.
- Any client-specific investment policy statement or risk tolerance.
- Trade surveillance or best-execution review on actual trades placed.

The bot is a tool that makes the advisor more efficient. The advisor remains the practitioner.
